SpaceTrash Hack: Revolutionizing Recycling on Mars

The Challenge: Sustainable Waste Management on Mars

Event: 2025 NASA Space Apps Challenge

Summary

A hypothetical 8-person crew on a Mars round-trip mission would accumulate **12,600 kg of inorganic waste** (packaging, textiles, structural materials) over three years. Transporting resources from Earth or trash back to Earth is expensive and inefficient. The core challenge is to **design sustainable systems to manage, reuse, or recycle this inorganic waste** on the Martian surface to achieve a circular economy.

Background

Astronauts cannot rely on returning waste to Earth like on the ISS. Future Mars missions must implement sanitation/recycling to process common waste items (fabrics, food packaging, foam, etc.) that are difficult to reuse in their original form. **Innovative, cutting-edge solutions** are needed to develop a recycling infrastructure for planetary missions, focusing on maximizing resource recovery and minimizing waste outputs.

Objectives: Design a Sustainable Recycling System for Jezero Crater

Design a system that processes waste to address one or more of the following scenarios, focusing on **immediate recycling/reuse solutions**:

  • Residence Renovations: How to reuse structural elements (aluminum, polymer composites) and foam packaging after habitat inflation.
  • Cosmic Celebrations: How to recycle everyday items like clothing, wipes, and food pouches for a celebratory event.
  • Daring Discoveries: How to reuse materials from scientific experiments, like EVA waste, filter instruments, and surplus carbon from oxygen extraction.

Key Focus: Maximize resource recovery, minimize energy/water inputs, and minimize unusable outputs. Avoid burning/incinerating waste, and prevent the generation of toxic emissions or microplastics.
